    SPIDER CRAB

    The Japanese Spider Crab is a large species of deep-sea crab that lives on the Pacific side of Japan as far south as Taiwan and at chilly depths ranging from 50m to as low as 500m

    The Japanese Spider Crab is a large species of deep-sea crab that lives on the Pacific side of Japan as far south as Taiwan and at chilly depths ranging from 50m to as low as 500m

    It is the largest crustacean in the world, with an armor measuring approximately 40cm. It has 10 long spindly legs with claws at the end of one pair that grow especially large and long for males. The largest specimen was 5,7m. These enormous animals start out tiny—females produce more than a million eggs that are about 0,7cm each.

    These slow-moving crabs don’t hunt, prefering to scavenge for dead animal or plant matter, though they may also eat live fish or invertebrates such as other crustaceans.

    This creature stays in one place and just moves around a bit. When poked, raise their front arms. To the people of Japan, the Japanese Spider Crab was known for being able to cause serious injuries with its strong front claws.
